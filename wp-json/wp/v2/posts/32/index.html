Smoke stemming from hundreds of burning\u00a0wildfires in Canada\u00a0has made its way to the northeastern U.S., where it is now clogging the air and causing a significant drop in air quality. New York City is now leading the ranks as the\u00a0world\u2019s most polluted city, with the worst air pollution readings seen in decades.<\/p>\n

Wildfire smoke is one of the\u00a0fastest-growing sources of air pollution in the United States. Particulate matter pollution from fires can cause significant health complications for those that are pregnant, young children, the elderly, outdoor workers, residents of leaky buildings and anyone with heart or lung ailments.<\/p>\n

MuckRock has spent the past two years working on various air pollution journalism projects, including\u00a0analyzing pollution data and installing our own air sensors in Chicago\u00a0and digging into\u00a0wildfire smoke data in California.<\/p>\n

Here is a collection of MuckRock resources, including a data archive of historic U.S. wildfires from the National InterAgency Fire Center; how-to guides on what type of documents and data to request from local governments; and live PurpleAir sensor data from Chicago and New York City to help newsrooms, civic groups and the public make sense of wildfire data.<\/p>\n

What documents and data to request about air pollution from your local government<\/p>\n

MuckRock\u2019s \u201cAir Quality Access\u201d series\u00a0offers guides to help you investigate air quality in your community using public records requests.<\/p>\n

<id=”create-request-button”>Explore MuckRock\u2019s “Air Quality Access” how-to guides for public records requests<\/id=”create-request-button”><\/p>\n

This guide includes advice on:<\/p>\n

Finding your local air pollution control board or air pollution branch of your state\u2019s environmental agency and\u00a0knowing what documents to ask for, including air pollution permits, annual air pollutant reports and new source review permits;<\/p>\n

Asking your local government\u00a0how it regulates polluters in your area, including requesting initial complaints, sustained violations and monitoring reports;<\/p>\n

Asking what your local government is\u00a0doing to improve your air quality\u00a0beyond issues of compliance or how polluters ended up in your community in the first place.<\/p>\n

Explore our EPA wildfire data, including historic U.S. wildfires<\/p>\n

Our\u00a0EPA wildfire data on GitHub\u00a0contains data and findings\u00a0that are part of an ongoing collaboration between MuckRock and the California Newsroom on the increasing impact of wildfire smoke on Californians\u2019 health. The data and analysis underpin the visualizations and findings in the story \u201cAs Wildfire Smoke Worsens Public Health, Government Watchdog Calls EPA Response \u2018Ad Hoc\u2019\u201d.<\/p>\n

MuckRock also\u00a0provides a GitHub repository\u00a0for data on the increasing impact of wildfire smoke on Californians\u2019 health.<\/p>\n

Additional MuckRock Resources<\/p>\n

\u201cWhat\u2019s in the air?\u201d\u00a0from MuckRock, WBEZ and the Chicago Sun-Times offers an illustrated explainer of particulate matter, which consists of very small particles suspended in the air we breathe. The explainer highlights how high levels of particulate matter can lead to health issues like asthma, heart attack or chronic obstructive pulmonary disease.<\/p>\n

How You Can Monitor Chicago\u2019s Air Pollution Hotspots on GitHub\u00a0contains tools to help Chicagoans stay up to date on the city\u2019s air quality and is part of an ongoing project by MuckRock.<\/p>\n

As part of its project to\u00a0monitor air quality in Chicago, MuckRock also\u00a0provides real-time readings from a PurpleAir sensor\u00a0in Cicero, which has some of the highest levels of particulate matter pollution.<\/p>\n
