A Norwegian cruise ship lost the ability to navigate after a rogue wave crashed into it Thursday, the cruise company HX said.<\/p>\n

The MS Maud lost power after the wave hit as the ship was sailing toward Tilbury, England, from Flor\u00f8, Norway, HX, a unit of Norway\u2019s Hurtigruten Group, said in a statement.<\/p>\n

None of the passengers or crew members were seriously injured, HX said.\u00a0Reuters\u00a0reported that all 266 passengers and 131 crew members are safe, citing the Danish Joint Rescue Coordination Centre.<\/p>\n

“The situation is stable, the ship has propulsion and they are able to navigate the ship manually via emergency systems,” the Danish Joint Rescue Coordination Centre said in a statement Friday local time.<\/p>\n

The rogue wave shattered windows on the ship’s bridge, which caused water to enter the vessel and resulted in a power outage, Reuters reported.<\/p>\n

HX said in the statement that the ship’s crew conducted “thorough safety checks and technical assessments” and that further checks were underway to ensure the ship could continue on course.<\/p>\n
