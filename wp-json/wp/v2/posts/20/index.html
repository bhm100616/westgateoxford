Former President Donald Trump called for a Republican to challenge Rep. Chip Roy, R-Texas, on Monday night, even though the filing deadline to enter the congressional contest passed last week.<\/p>\n

Roy is running unopposed in the Republican primary in Texas\u2019 21st district in March after no other candidate filed ahead of the Dec. 11 deadline.<\/p>\n

Trump\u2019s late-night Truth Social post came after Florida Gov.\u00a0Ron DeSantis\u00a0appeared on Fox News alongside Roy, who has been traversing Iowa with the GOP presidential contender during his congressional holiday break.<\/p>\n

\u201cPeople are thirsty for a new generation and new vision. Looking forward, not looking backwards, somebody who can serve for eight years,\u201d Roy said on Fox News.<\/p>\n

Roy, a member of the conservative House Freedom Caucus, was the first member of Congress to endorse DeSantis\u2019 presidential bid back in March.<\/p>\n

DeSantis praised Roy for having \u201cone of the most consistent and strong conservative records\u201d when asked about Trump\u2019s comments during a campaign stop in Iowa on Tuesday.<\/p>\n

\u201cI do think it shows that you know, with Trump, it\u2019s all about if you kiss the ring,\u201d DeSantis said, noting that the former president has attacked another one of his prominent supporters, Iowa Gov. Kim Reynolds. \u201cAttacking Chip, just because he\u2019s supporting me, even though these are both people that have done really good work.\u201d<\/p>\n
